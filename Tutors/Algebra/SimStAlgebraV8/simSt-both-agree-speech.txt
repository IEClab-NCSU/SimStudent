;; While learning something we often ask ourselves what would happen if the situation was something else or what would happen if we would have taken another
;; alternative action. We bring in unknown things in the picture and try to apply our known ideas in those situation to make sure what we learned so far is
;; robust. This aim of the questions asked when both tutor tutee agree on a <i> is to help tutor create unknown/complex/similar example situation and apply
;; known concepts to those situation to make their knowledge robust. Therefore, people in any domain should design questions in this route
;; in a way that prompt tutor to create unknown/complex/similar situation and apply known knowledge to those situation.
;; For example: in our linear eq solving domain, Can you state an equation where I might have to add a negative number and explain how adding a negative
;;number would work for that equation? Prompting tutor to create an equation where add with negative number would apply and asking him to explain the
;;application to make sense of the solution step.
